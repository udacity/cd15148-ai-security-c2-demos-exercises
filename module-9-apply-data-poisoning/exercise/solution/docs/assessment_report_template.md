## Findings

The label-flipping attack intentionally corrupts part of the training supervision for one operationally important source class. The most important indicator is not only aggregate validation accuracy, but whether clean source-class signs are increasingly predicted as the attacker-selected target class.

## Operational Risk

If a poisoned model learns to confuse `Stop` and `Yield`, an autonomous shuttle may respond incorrectly to high-priority traffic control signs even when the camera input is clean. This is a training pipeline integrity failure rather than an inference-time perturbation problem.

## Mitigations

Recommended mitigations include dataset provenance controls, label review for safety-critical classes, class-conditional validation, anomaly checks for label distribution shifts, protected training pipelines, and model comparison gates before deployment.
